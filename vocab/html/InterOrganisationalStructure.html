dc:description
  • (en) In CERIF terms, the member role happens e.g. within the relationships person-organisation, person-project; organisation-organisation, that is, the classification term (cfTerm) "Member" identified by its cfClassificationIdentifier (a uuid), and reused with e.g. the cfPerson_OrganisationUnit; cfProject_Person; cfOrganisationUnit_OrganisationUnit relationships. A person can be a member of multiple organisations. An organization can be a member of another organization, or a state that belongs to a group of nations.

  • (en) A member is anything that belongs to a set or class.

Merger

https://w3id.org/cerif/vocab/Inter-OrganisationalStructure#Merger

rdf:type
  • https://w3id.org/cerif/model#cfClassification
  • http://www.w3.org/2004/02/skos/core#Concept
skos:topConceptOf
  • Inter-Organisational Structure vocabulary
dc:identifier
  • eda28bc7-34c5-11e1-b86c-0800200c9a66
rdfs:label
  • (en) Merger
skos:definition
  • (en) A merger is a voluntary amalgamation of two firms on roughly equal terms into one new legal entity. Mergers are effected by exchange of the pre-merger stock (shares) for the stock of the new firm. Owners of each pre-merger firm continue as owners, and the resources of the merging entities are pooled for the benefit of the new entity. If the merged entities were competitors, the merger is called horizontal integration, if they were supplier or customer of one another, it is called vertical integration.
dc:source
  • (en) CERIF Task Group
